# Postmortem — A5 Wallet Prototype and Book-First Structure

Date: 2026-06-27

Status: Locked design-system update

## Trigger

The physical A5 wallet/sleeve prototype showed that the card display hardware takes up more page space than expected.

The earlier idea of placing meaningful text beside the card is no longer viable on A5.

The gutter/spine allowance, page margins, sleeve/wallet size, and premium spacing requirements mean the card page must be treated as a centred display page.

## Core finding

The card page cannot do three jobs at once.

It cannot be:

- a premium display page
- a readable text page
- a safe/comfortable card-wallet page

Trying to make it do all three weakens the product.

## Locked correction

ApexCrest must be built as a complete illustrated book first.

The card display page becomes one participation/display moment inside a wider world chapter.

The book must work without cards inserted.

## New first-edition structure

Each world uses 4 pages arranged as 2 double-page spreads.

### Spread 1 — Book-first spread

Page 1: Quiet world entry page

- world title
- world subtitle/place name
- symbol/crest treatment
- restrained world-specific art, texture, border, or atmosphere
- no card language
- no prompt
- no writing field

Page 2: Authored world reading page

- standalone prose
- describes the place, feeling, atmosphere, and emotional landscape
- must have value without any card inserted
- no user-completion elements
- no card-selection instruction

### Spread 2 — Hero display spread

Page 3: Full world illustration page

- cinematic emotional landscape
- full world reveal
- strong enough to work as an illustrated book page with no card present

Page 4: Centred card display page

- centred wallet/display area
- minimal title/subtitle only if needed
- subtle world echo and premium border/texture
- no side text
- no long prompt
- no writing field
- no storage/inventory wording

## Why this order won

The earlier option of putting an echo page opposite the card reduced the impact of the card moment.

The strongest customer and marketing spread is:

**full world illustration on the left + centred card display page on the right.**

This keeps the “money shot” intact while still opening the chapter with book-first content.

Final hierarchy:

1. Spread 1 proves the world is authored book content.
2. Spread 2 delivers the emotional/card-display hero moment.

## Visual system correction

The first spread must not feel like a different book.

The quiet opening spread may reduce intensity, but it must not change style.

Each world has two registers:

### Quiet register

- restrained
- readable
- premium
- world-specific
- used on entry/text pages

### Hero register

- cinematic
- atmospheric
- higher intensity
- used on illustration/card spread

The difference is volume, not language.

For Ember, the quiet register should still use:

- ash
- charcoal
- ember flecks
- copper linework
- faint volcanic forms
- smoke
- dark warm texture

It must not become generic parchment fantasy styling.

## Prompt and writing-field decision

No prompts.

No writing lines.

No memory fields.

No fill-in areas.

No diary, scrapbook, record-book, workbook, checklist, or stationery behaviour.

Reason:

- it cheapens the book
- it weakens the VAT-safe book-first argument
- it creates problems when the customer changes the card
- it makes the product feel incomplete until written in

The book uses authored text only.

## VAT-safe implications

This structure strengthens the book-first position because:

- only 1 page per 4-page world is card-display-led
- the other 3 pages are authored/illustrated book content
- the book remains valuable without cards
- the first edition can target 48–56 pages
- 10 card display pages remain comfortably below 25% of total pages when the book reaches 48–56 pages

Preferred first-edition page count:

- 56 pages total
- 10 card display pages
- card display pages = 17.9% of total pages

Acceptable working range:

- 48–56 pages total
- 10 card display pages maximum
- card display pages below 25% of total pages

Avoid:

- 20-page structure
- 40-page structure
- any structure where 10 card pages represent 25% or more of the book

## Public-facing language lock

Use:

- premium illustrated collector-world book
- illustrated collector book
- book of emotional worlds
- personal card display page
- display page
- crest page
- personal display space

Avoid:

- album
- binder
- holder
- wallet book
- storage book
- card organiser
- card sleeve book
- scrapbook
- record book
- workbook

## Final locked rule

Book first.

World first.

Card second.

The card can still be visually powerful when inserted, but the product must remain a finished illustrated book without it.
