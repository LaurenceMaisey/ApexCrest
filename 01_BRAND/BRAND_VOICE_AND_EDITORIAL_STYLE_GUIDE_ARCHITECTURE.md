# Brand Voice & Editorial Style Guide Architecture

## Status

Architecture v1.0.

This is a Level 1 Governance Document for the ApexCrest brand voice system.

It defines the required structure for the canonical Brand Voice & Editorial Style Guide before the full guide is drafted.

## Purpose

This architecture prevents the final guide from becoming a loose style note.

The final guide must become the editorial operating system for ApexCrest across:

- books
- packaging
- website
- product pages
- marketplaces
- social media
- email
- customer support
- founder content
- compatibility wording
- future products

## Governance position

Once created and locked, the canonical Brand Voice & Editorial Style Guide should be treated as normative.

If another ApexCrest document contains conflicting editorial guidance, the Brand Voice & Editorial Style Guide should take precedence unless a later approved revision explicitly supersedes it.

---

# Required Architecture

## 1. Purpose & Scope

Define what the guide governs and what it does not govern.

Must clarify that it controls ApexCrest written voice across all brand, product and customer-facing contexts.

## 2. Design Principles

Define the principles used to build the voice system.

Must include:

- evidence-led, not intuition-led
- collector-native, not franchise-imitative
- book-first, not storage-first
- emotionally premium, not fake-luxury
- clear enough for mass-market use

## 3. Brand Positioning

Define how ApexCrest speaks from its strategic position.

Must reinforce:

- premium illustrated collector book
- 10 worlds / 10 chosen cards
- meaning before value
- emotional landscapes
- for the cards that earned their place

## 4. Reader Psychology

Define the target reader and emotional needs.

Must address:

- collectors who value cards for meaning
- collectors who upgrade and replace cards over time
- collectors across multiple TCGs
- gift buyers
- premium product buyers
- parents/families where relevant

## 5. Brand Archetype Reconciliation

Review and reconcile previous archetype thinking against the voice research.

Current recommended archetype structure:

- Primary: Curator
- Secondary: Sage
- Tertiary: Explorer

Purpose:

- Curator selects and honours.
- Sage explains and gives calm judgement.
- Explorer opens the worlds and invites choice.

Must also explicitly record that Ruler and Magician are not core archetypes unless deliberately reopened later.

## 6. Voice Philosophy

Define the central philosophy of the voice.

Recommended centre:

**A beautifully illustrated collector book written by people who understand why certain cards matter.**

## 7. Voice DNA

Define the measurable voice qualities.

Must include:

- warmth
- reverence
- collector fluency
- wonder
- beauty
- nostalgia
- legacy
- practical clarity
- low hype
- no franchise imitation
- no pretension

## 8. Emotional Register

Define what the voice should make readers feel.

Must include:

- respected
- understood
- welcomed
- inspired
- nostalgic
- confident in choosing their own cards

Must avoid making readers feel:

- sold to
- shouted at
- excluded
- overwhelmed with lore
- pressured to use only rare/valuable cards

## 9. Editorial Principles

Convert the voice philosophy into rules.

Must include:

- Meaning before value
- Worlds before slots
- Emotion before mechanics
- Illustration before card
- Plain English before ornate prose
- Quiet confidence before hype
- Ownable language before franchise familiarity

## 10. Language Architecture

Define how vocabulary is organised.

Must include a tiered system:

- core vocabulary
- supporting vocabulary
- controlled vocabulary
- avoid vocabulary

## 11. Canonical Vocabulary

Define words ApexCrest should use confidently.

Likely examples:

- world
- place
- earned
- meaning
- card
- collection
- collector
- chosen
- kept
- story
- memory
- wonder
- beauty
- legacy
- honour
- Crest
- Crested
- Crest-worthy

## 12. Vocabulary to Use Carefully

Define useful but context-sensitive words.

Likely examples:

- legendary
- adventure
- treasure
- iconic
- power
- triumph
- archive
- vault
- dream
- companion
- transformation

## 13. Vocabulary to Avoid

Define banned or high-risk terms.

Must include:

- franchise-coded terms
- slogan-adjacent language
- role names from specific TCGs
- storage-first words
- album/binder framing where possible

## 14. Sentence Rhythm & Cadence

Define how ApexCrest sentences move.

Must include the recommended four-beat rhythm:

1. short emotional observation
2. expanded truth
3. connection to book/world/card
4. quiet closing line

## 15. Grammar & Style Rules

Define house style.

Must include:

- UK English default
- plain sentence structure
- restrained punctuation
- limited exclamation marks
- no excessive fantasy capitalisation
- no forced lore terminology

## 16. Book Voice

Define in-book editorial copy.

Must be the most poetic and restrained channel.

Must support book-first/VAT-conscious positioning.

## 17. Packaging Voice

Define front cover, back cover, belly band, sticker and insert copy.

Must be short, confident and book-first.

## 18. Website Voice

Define homepage and website content.

Must be clearer and more practical than in-book copy, while retaining brand tone.

## 19. Product Description Voice

Define product-page language.

Must explain what the product is, who it is for, how it works and why it matters.

## 20. Retail Marketplace Voice

Define Amazon, Etsy, Shopify and similar marketplace copy.

Must be:

- searchable
- clear
- legally careful
- still premium
- not storage-first

## 21. Social Media Voice

Define social hooks, captions, reels, launch content and community prompts.

Must be immediate without becoming hype-led.

## 22. Email Voice

Define email launches, updates, abandoned basket, customer follow-up and newsletter tone.

Must be warm, clear and restrained.

## 23. Customer Support Voice

Define practical support tone.

Must prioritise clarity over mythology.

## 24. Founder Voice

Define how founders speak in behind-the-scenes content.

Must preserve premium brand perception while allowing authenticity.

## 25. Compatibility Language Rules

Define how ApexCrest may explain card compatibility.

Must separate compatibility language from brand voice.

Must avoid making franchise names part of the product identity.

## 26. Legal & Trade Mark Guardrails

Define language rules that reduce confusion, affiliation and franchise-imitation risk.

Must avoid:

- official slogans
- logos
- characters
- set names
- role names
- mechanic names
- confusing similarity

## 27. Book-First & VAT-Supportive Language Rules

Define language that supports the product as an illustrated collector book.

Must prefer:

- illustrated collector book
- world
- story
- page
- meaning
- chosen cards
- reading experience

Must avoid leading with:

- storage
- holder
- mount
- insert
- slot
- album
- binder
- display accessory

## 28. Crest / Crested / Crest-worthy Canon

Define how ApexCrest uses its own coined language.

Must explain:

- Crest
- Crested
- Cresting
- Crest-worthy
- un-Crest / remove / replace wording

Must avoid overuse or making the product sound like a card-mounting system.

## 29. Naming & Tagline Rules

Define how to use:

- ApexCrest
- ApexCrest Book
- world names
- product edition names
- For the cards that earned their place

Must clarify hierarchy between brand name, product name, editions and taglines.

## 30. Example Library

Provide examples of copy that sounds like ApexCrest.

Must include short, medium and long examples.

## 31. Rewrite Library

Provide before/after transformations.

Must show how to convert:

- storage copy into book-first copy
- hype copy into quiet confidence
- value-first copy into meaning-first copy
- franchise-coded copy into ApexCrest copy

## 32. Content Production System

Provide reusable production frameworks for future content.

Must include templates for:

- in-book world intros
- in-book transition pages
- intro pages
- closing pages
- product pages
- back cover copy
- packaging stickers
- Amazon/Etsy/Shopify listings
- Kickstarter pages
- social media posts
- reel scripts
- launch emails
- customer support replies
- FAQ entries
- compatibility disclaimers
- retail descriptions
- founder videos
- seasonal campaigns
- new world/product launches

## 33. ApexCrest Language Test

Create a diagnostic test for all new copy.

Must ask:

- Does this sound like ApexCrest?
- Could it belong to Pokémon, Magic or Lorcana instead?
- Does it reinforce book-first positioning?
- Does it prioritise meaning over storage?
- Is it emotionally authentic rather than promotional?
- Can collectors of multiple TCGs read it naturally?
- Is it legally and commercially clean?

## 34. Approval Checklist

Create a pass/fail checklist for all customer-facing copy.

Must cover:

- voice fit
- legal safety
- VAT-supportive wording
- cross-franchise neutrality
- clarity
- premium restraint
- collector authenticity

## 35. Governance & Change Control

Define how the guide is updated.

Must include:

- versioning
- change reasons
- approval process
- conflict resolution
- relationship to other ApexCrest documents

## 36. Version History

Record guide versions and update notes.

# Architecture Readiness Assessment

## Completeness

This architecture covers brand voice, product voice, channel voice, legal safety, VAT-conscious positioning, repeatable content production, governance and future scalability.

## Mass-market readiness

The architecture is designed to support copy that is clear enough for mainstream buyers while remaining credible to serious collectors.

## Cross-franchise readiness

The architecture prevents ApexCrest from sounding like any single TCG brand while preserving shared collector resonance.

## Commercial scalability

The Content Production System ensures the guide can generate repeatable content for future campaigns, listings, emails, reels, packaging and launches.

## Final judgement

This architecture is ready to control the drafting of the canonical Brand Voice & Editorial Style Guide.

No further architecture sections should be added unless a specific, material gap is identified during drafting.
