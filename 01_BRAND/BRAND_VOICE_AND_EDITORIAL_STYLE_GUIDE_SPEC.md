# Brand Voice & Editorial Style Guide Specification

## Status

Specification v1.0.

This file defines the required workflow, research method, output structure, audit criteria and legal guardrails for creating the ApexCrest Brand Voice & Editorial Style Guide.

The guide itself must not be created until the research and synthesis stages have been completed and recorded.

## Purpose

The purpose of the Brand Voice & Editorial Style Guide is to define a distinct ApexCrest voice that can work across fantasy trading-card communities without copying, imitating or relying on any one franchise.

The final voice must feel familiar to collectors while remaining legally clean, ownable, premium and recognisably ApexCrest.

## Core objective

Create a voice that is:

- collector-native
- emotionally premium
- lightly mythic
- legally clean
- warm and reverent
- flexible across fantasy TCGs
- compatible with book-first positioning
- distinct from Pokémon, Magic: The Gathering, Disney Lorcana, Yu-Gi-Oh!, One Piece and other existing brands

## Non-objective

The objective is not to make ApexCrest sound like Pokémon, Magic, Lorcana, Yu-Gi-Oh! or any other franchise.

The objective is to identify the shared emotional language of fantasy TCG collectors, then build an original ApexCrest voice from that territory.

## Research dimensions

Each brand/community must be analysed across the following dimensions.

### 1. Official brand voice

Assess tone, cadence, emotional register, product language, world language, promotional copy and official naming patterns.

### 2. Collector community voice

Assess how collectors actually talk about cards, collecting, pulls, chases, rarity, favourite cards, memories, trades, displays and grails.

### 3. Emotional territory

Identify the dominant emotional appeals, such as:

- wonder
- nostalgia
- power
- mastery
- discovery
- beauty
- chase
- achievement
- friendship
- rivalry
- legacy
- belonging

### 4. Cadence and sentence rhythm

Assess whether the language is:

- short and punchy
- ceremonial
- playful
- lore-heavy
- competitive
- cinematic
- nostalgic
- collector-practical

### 5. Vocabulary clusters

Identify common words that are safe, generic or potentially useful.

Examples to test:

- collection
- collector
- card
- rare
- world
- story
- memory
- legacy
- chase
- favourite
- showcase
- honour
- place
- journey
- reveal
- pull
- treasure
- vault
- archive
- display
- set
- edition
- legend
- wonder

### 6. Legally sensitive language

Identify terms that are franchise-coded, slogan-adjacent, trademark-sensitive, or likely to imply affiliation.

These must not become ApexCrest brand language.

Examples to investigate and likely avoid:

- catch / catch them all style language
- trainer
- Poké-style compounds
- mana
- planeswalker
- spellbook / spellcasting if Magic-coded in context
- duel / duelist if Yu-Gi-Oh-coded in context
- enchanted if Disney-coded in context
- official slogans
- set names
- faction names
- character names
- franchise-specific role names

### 7. Compatibility with ApexCrest

Score whether each brand/community voice can inform ApexCrest without creating legal, tonal or positioning risk.

## Scoring rubric

1. Emotional relevance
2. Collector-language relevance
3. Premium compatibility

5. Legal cleanliness

## Required legal guardrails

The final voice must not:

- copy slogans
- imitate franchise packaging language
- imitate franchise role language
- imply affiliation, sponsorship or endorsement
- use character names as brand language
- use franchise-specific terminology as ApexCrest terminology
- rely on compatibility with one named franchise
- sound like an unofficial expansion of any existing TCG

The guide may allow careful descriptive compatibility language, but this must be separated from brand voice.

## Required VAT / book-first guardrails

The voice must support ApexCrest as an illustrated collector book, not as a card album or storage accessory.

Prefer language such as:

- illustrated collector book
- world
- story
- place
- meaning
- honour
- selected
- chosen
- kept

Avoid leading with:

- album
- binder
- storage
- holder
- insert cards
- card slots
- mount cards
- display accessory

## Required validation tests

The final guide must pass the following tests.

### 1. Cross-TCG test

The copy must feel natural to collectors of Pokémon, Magic, Lorcana, Yu-Gi-Oh!, One Piece and other fantasy TCGs.

### 2. Franchise-distance test

The copy must not sound as though it belongs to any single franchise.

### 3. Collector-authenticity test

The copy must sound like it understands collectors, not like generic gift-product marketing.

### 4. Premium-restraint test

The copy must feel premium without becoming pompous, ornate or fake-luxury.

### 5. Book-first test

The copy must reinforce the product as a book with worlds and meaning, not a card storage product.

### 6. Plain-English test

The copy must remain clear, readable and emotionally direct.

### 7. Ownability test

The voice should make phrases like Crest, Crested, Crest-worthy and For the cards that earned their place feel natural and ownable.

## Failure criteria

The final guide fails if the voice becomes:

- too Pokémon-coded
- too Magic-coded
- too Disney-coded
- too Yu-Gi-Oh-coded
- too fantasy-novel
- too game-manual
- too corporate
- too childish
- too hype-driven
- too vague
- too sentimental
- too legally risky
- too focused on card storage rather than book value
