# 25. Compatibility Language Rules

## Purpose

Compatibility language explains which cards ApexCrest can physically work with without making another brand part of the ApexCrest identity.

Compatibility copy must be clear enough for buyers, but secondary to the product's own identity as an illustrated collector book.

## Core rule

Lead with ApexCrest.

Clarify compatibility second.

Never lead with a third-party franchise name unless the context is a search field, FAQ, customer support answer or legal-safe compatibility clarification.

## Preferred compatibility frame

Use:

> Designed for standard-size trading cards.

Use:

> Fits most standard TCG cards when sleeved appropriately.

Use:

> Cards are not included.

Use:

> Unofficial collector book. Not affiliated with, endorsed by, or sponsored by any card game publisher.

## Avoid as brand identity

Avoid making the front cover, core tagline or main product name depend on phrases such as:

- Pokémon book
- Magic book
- Lorcana book
- Yu-Gi-Oh book
- card binder for Pokémon
- TCG holder for Magic

## Acceptable contexts for franchise names

Franchise names may be used carefully in:

- FAQ answers
- marketplace search fields where legally reviewed
- compatibility pages
- customer support replies
- removable compatibility labels if legally approved

Use only to describe compatibility, not affiliation.

## Compatibility hierarchy

Use this order:

1. ApexCrest product identity
2. Standard-size trading card compatibility
3. Cards not included
4. Non-affiliation statement
5. Specific franchise names only if necessary and legally safe

## Example

Strong:

> ApexCrest is an illustrated collector book designed for standard-size trading cards. It is an unofficial product and is not affiliated with any card game publisher. Cards are not included.

Weak:

> The perfect Pokémon, Magic and Lorcana card book.

# 26. Legal & Trade Mark Guardrails

## Purpose

These guardrails reduce the risk of confusion, implied endorsement, franchise imitation and trade mark misuse.

This section is not legal advice. It is an editorial safety system. Formal legal review should be sought before packaging, marketplace listings and paid advertising go live.

## Legal posture

ApexCrest must behave as an independent product.

It may support collectors who own many types of cards, but it must not look, sound or present itself as an official accessory, expansion or companion product for any existing franchise.

## Do not use

Do not use:

- third-party logos
- official slogans
- official character names as brand copy
- official card set names as product identity
- faction, role, class or mechanic names from specific games
- official visual frames or card layouts
- confusingly similar typography
- slogan-adjacent phrasing
- franchise-coloured design cues that imply affiliation

## Non-affiliation copy

Plain non-affiliation wording should be used where relevant.

Example:

> ApexCrest is an unofficial collector book. It is not affiliated with, endorsed by, or sponsored by any card game publisher. All trade marks are the property of their respective owners.

## Tone of disclaimers

Disclaimers must be plain, separate and functional.

Do not make legal disclaimers poetic.

Weak:

> ApexCrest walks its own path beyond the kingdoms of other card worlds.

Strong:

> ApexCrest is an independent, unofficial collector book.

## Highest-risk areas

Review carefully before publication:

- front cover
- product title
- removable compatibility stickers
- Amazon title and bullets
- paid social ads
- Google Shopping copy
- packaging back-cover disclaimer
- influencer scripts

# 27. Book-First & VAT-Supportive Language Rules

## Purpose

ApexCrest language must support the product as an illustrated collector book, not as a storage accessory.

This section is not tax advice. It defines editorial discipline that supports the current book-first product strategy.

## Core rule

Describe ApexCrest first as:

> an illustrated collector book

Do not describe it first as:

> a card holder

## Preferred language

Use:

- illustrated collector book
- book
- pages
- worlds
- emotional worlds
- chosen cards
- reading experience
- illustrated spreads
- meaning
- story
- collector book

## Avoid leading with

Avoid leading with:

- storage
- binder
- album
- holder
- mounting system
- display accessory
- insert pack
- card slots
- pockets
- protection

These may be used only where practical clarity requires them.

## Book completeness rule

The book must be described as complete before cards are added.

Strong:

> ApexCrest is an illustrated collector book with ten emotional worlds for ten chosen cards.

Weak:

> ApexCrest becomes complete once you fill all ten card slots.

## Card relationship rule

The card should be framed as entering a page or world, not filling a blank.

Strong:

> Choose the card that belongs in this world.

Weak:

> Insert a card into the empty space.

## VAT-sensitive watch point

Avoid copy that makes the physical card-holding function appear to be the main product.

Marketplace copy may require functional terms, but the opening line, product identity and brand explanation must remain book-first.

# 28. Crest / Crested / Crest-worthy Canon

## Purpose

Crest language gives ApexCrest its own vocabulary.

It should create distinctiveness without becoming gimmicky, confusing or overused.

## Core meanings

### Crest

A recognised place of honour within ApexCrest.

Also used as a verb meaning to choose a card for one of the worlds.

### Crested

A card that has been chosen and placed within ApexCrest.

### Cresting

The act of choosing and placing a card into a world.

Use sparingly.

### Crest-worthy

A card that has earned consideration for a place.

Not necessarily rare, expensive or objectively superior.

## Correct use

Strong:

> Not every card needs to be Crested.

Strong:

> Crest-worthy cards are chosen by meaning, memory, artwork, rarity or instinct.

Strong:

> A card can become Crest-worthy later as your collection changes.

## Avoid overuse

Weak:

> Start Cresting your Crest-worthy cards into Crested worlds with the ApexCrest Crest system.

## Replace/remove wording

Preferred language:

- replace a card
- change a card
- move a card
- remove a card
- choose again
- re-Crest, only if the meaning is clear

Use `un-Crest` only as internal or experimental language unless tested with real customers.

## Canon rule

Crest language should support meaning.

It should never make ApexCrest sound like a mechanical card-mounting system.

# 29. Naming & Tagline Rules

## Brand name

Use:

> ApexCrest

Do not shorten to Apex unless explicitly discussing internal naming history.

## Product naming hierarchy

Use this hierarchy:

1. Brand: ApexCrest
2. Product type: Illustrated Collector Book
3. Edition or product name, if applicable
4. World names
5. Tagline

## Core product descriptor

Preferred:

> ApexCrest Illustrated Collector Book

Acceptable variants:

- ApexCrest Collector Book
- ApexCrest Book
- ApexCrest: 10 Worlds for 10 Chosen Cards

## Core tagline

Use:

> For the cards that earned their place.

This line should remain stable unless a later approved positioning review supersedes it.

## Supporting lines

Acceptable:

- Ten worlds. Ten chosen cards.
- Choose the world that feels right.
- A collector book for cards that mean more.
- Not every card needs to be Crested.

## World names

World names should remain emotionally clear and visually distinct.

Use the locked world language from the visual/world system.

Avoid renaming worlds casually in marketing copy.

## Naming rule

Names must increase clarity, not create lore burden.

ApexCrest is a premium collector book, not a fantasy franchise encyclopedia.

# 30. Example Library

## Purpose

The Example Library provides model copy that sounds like ApexCrest.

Examples should be reused as references, not copied blindly into every context.

## Short examples

> Some cards stay with us.

> Not every card needs to be Crested.

> Choose the world that feels right.

> For the cards that earned their place.

> Ten worlds. Ten chosen cards.

## Medium examples

> Every collection has cards that feel different. ApexCrest gives ten of them a world of their own.

> A card can be chosen for rarity, artwork, memory or instinct. What matters is that it earned its place.

> ApexCrest is not a binder for everything. It is a collector book for the cards that mean more.

## Long example

> Some cards are kept for reasons only the collector understands. They may not be the rarest, the newest or the most valuable. They simply carry something with them. ApexCrest gives those cards a world of their own.

## Product explanation example

> ApexCrest is an illustrated collector book with ten emotional worlds for ten chosen cards. Each world offers a different feeling, from passion and memory to challenge, wonder and achievement. Choose the card that belongs there, and change it later as your collection grows.

## Compatibility example

> Designed for standard-size trading cards. Cards are not included. ApexCrest is an independent, unofficial collector book and is not affiliated with any card game publisher.

## Purpose

The Rewrite Library shows how to transform weak or risky copy into ApexCrest copy.

## Storage-first to book-first

Weak:

> Holds ten cards in premium display slots.

> Ten worlds for ten chosen cards.

Weak:

> The ultimate must-have collector experience.

ApexCrest:

> A collector book for the cards that earned their place.

Weak:

> Show off your rarest and most expensive cards.

ApexCrest:

Weak:

> Catch your favourites and evolve your collection.

# 32. Content Production System

## Purpose

The Content Production System gives future ApexCrest copy a repeatable starting point.

It prevents every listing, caption, email and script from being reinvented from scratch.

## Universal copy sequence

Most ApexCrest copy should follow this order:

1. Recognition
2. Meaning
3. World/book connection
4. Practical clarity
5. Quiet action

## Product-page template

1. Emotional headline
2. One-sentence product definition
3. What is included
4. How the 10-world system works
5. Compatibility note
6. Flexibility/replacement reassurance
7. Non-affiliation note
8. Call to action

## In-book world intro template

1. Name the feeling
2. Suggest the atmosphere
3. Invite the collector to choose
4. Leave space for interpretation

## Social post template

1. Recognition hook
2. Image/page/world focus
3. Simple collector question
4. Light product identifier

## Email template

1. Recognition line
2. Update or reason for writing
3. Why it matters
4. Clear next step
5. Calm sign-off

## Customer support template

1. Direct answer
2. Practical detail
3. Reassurance
4. Next step

## Founder video template

1. The problem with ordinary storage
2. The idea behind ApexCrest
3. The 10-world system
4. Why choice matters
5. What happens next

# 33. ApexCrest Language Test

## Purpose

The Language Test is a diagnostic tool for any new ApexCrest copy.

Copy should not be published if it fails materially.

## Test questions

Ask:

1. Does this sound like ApexCrest?
2. Could this accidentally sound like Pokémon, Magic, Lorcana, Yu-Gi-Oh!, One Piece or another franchise?
3. Does this reinforce book-first positioning?
4. Does it prioritise meaning over storage?
5. Does it avoid making price the only measure of value?
6. Can collectors of different TCGs read it naturally?
7. Is it clear to a non-expert buyer?
8. Is compatibility secondary to product identity?
9. Is any legal disclaimer plain and separate?
10. Does the copy feel calm, premium and human?

## Pass standard

ApexCrest copy should pass at least eight of the ten questions.

Any failure on legal safety, franchise imitation or book-first positioning must be corrected before publication.

# 34. Approval Checklist

## Purpose

The Approval Checklist governs customer-facing ApexCrest copy before release.

## Required checks

Before publication, confirm:

- voice fits ApexCrest
- meaning leads the copy
- book-first language is preserved
- functional terms are secondary
- franchise names are not part of product identity
- no slogans or slogan-adjacent lines are used
- legal disclaimers are plain and separate
- compatibility is clear but controlled
- UK English is used unless context requires otherwise
- copy is understandable on first reading
- no unnecessary hype is present
- no over-poetic language weakens clarity

## High-risk copy requiring extra review

Extra review is required for:

- packaging
- Amazon titles
- marketplace bullets
- paid adverts
- compatibility stickers
- influencer scripts
- press releases
- trade mark-facing copy

## Approval rule

If copy is commercially useful but legally or strategically risky, it must be rewritten.

ApexCrest should not borrow risk to gain short-term familiarity.

# 35. Governance & Change Control

## Purpose

Governance prevents voice drift as ApexCrest grows.

## Normative structure

The Brand Voice & Editorial Style Guide is canonical when read as:

- the index file
- Part 01 Foundations
- Part 02 Language Engine
- Part 03 Channel Rules
- Part 04 Legal, Book-First and Governance Rules

## Conflict rule

If another ApexCrest document conflicts with this guide on writing style, naming, tone or editorial positioning, this guide takes precedence unless a later approved document explicitly supersedes it.

## Change process

Material changes require:

1. A proposed change note
2. Reason for change
3. Risk assessment
4. No-loss review against existing guidance
5. Approval before merge

## Versioning

Use semantic draft status:

- v0.x for draft phases
- v1.0 for first locked canonical guide
- v1.x for approved additions
- v2.0 for major strategic changes

## Archive rule

Companion files, research files and integration plans are evidence.

They do not override canonical part files unless explicitly promoted.
