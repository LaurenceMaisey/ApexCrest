# Crest Language Lock

## Status

Locked public language rule.

Date locked: 2026-06-27

## Locked line

**Some cards deserve more than a binder. They deserve a Crest.**

## Why this line is locked

This line is the preferred public-facing ApexCrest positioning line because it does three jobs at once:

1. it separates ApexCrest from binders, albums, storage folders, and ordinary card-holder products;
2. it gives the product an emotional/status claim rather than a storage claim;
3. it lets **Crest** carry a deliberate double meaning.

## Double meaning of Crest

In this line, **a Crest** means both:

1. the ApexCrest book/product; and
2. a symbolic mark of honour, recognition, earned place, and significance.

This is intentional.

The phrase should point towards the book as a premium object, not merely towards the act of inserting a card.

## Correct hierarchy

The public language hierarchy is:

1. book/product identity
2. emotional recognition
3. illustrated world experience
4. optional personal card display

It must not become:

1. card insertion
2. swapping/upgrading cards
3. storage capacity
4. album/holder functionality

## Safe use

Use:

- Some cards deserve more than a binder. They deserve a Crest.
- A Crest is a place of recognition, not storage.
- A Crest marks the cards that earned their place.
- ApexCrest is a premium illustrated collector-world book.
- The card display pages are personal display moments inside the book.

## Avoid

Avoid official/customer-facing use of language that makes Crest sound like a mechanical card-handling verb.

Do not lead with:

- crest your card
- insert your card
- re-crest
- un-crest
- up-crest
- down-crest
- swap your crested cards
- upgrade your crested cards
- organise your crested cards
- stores 10 crested cards

These phrases may emerge as user slang, but they should not be official product, packaging, advert, influencer-brief, or listing language unless reviewed.

## VAT defensibility explanation

The line supports the VAT-safe strategy because it creates contrast against binders and card-storage products.

It frames ApexCrest as a premium illustrated book and symbolic recognition object, not as a card storage or card-mounting system.

However, wording alone does not determine VAT treatment. The VAT position still depends on the final physical product, its primary function, the amount of standalone authored/illustrated book content, the proportion of card display pages, the presence and function of wallets/pockets, packaging, listings, marketing, and customer-facing instructions.

## VAT caveat

This file is a brand and design guardrail, not tax advice.

A written VAT specialist opinion is still required before relying on zero-rating.

## Final rule

**Crest = book identity + symbolic recognition.**

**Crest must not mean storage, organising, swapping, or mechanical insertion.**
