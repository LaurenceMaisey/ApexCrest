# Book Structure

## Status

Locked current first-edition structure, updated after the A5 wallet prototype review, 64-page book-map decision, ritual-anatomy lock, and World Crest / feeling-line refinement on 2026-06-27.

Names are currently a **working set**, not final manuscript locks.

## Core structure

**10 Worlds. 10 Crests. 64-page preferred first edition.**

The first ApexCrest collector book must contain one centred personal Crest/display page per world.

The Crest page is a symbolic recognition moment inside a complete illustrated world chapter. It is not the chapter itself.

This keeps the product:

- physically practical
- visually premium
- emotionally focused
- easier to manufacture
- stronger as a book-first/VAT-safe product
- clearer for customers to understand
- less likely to read as a binder, album, holder, storage book, or stationery product

## Prototype finding

The A5 card wallet/sleeve prototype proved that meaningful side text cannot sit neatly beside the card.

The gutter/spine space plus the actual wallet size means the centred Crest/display page must be treated as a dedicated visual page, not a mixed text-and-card page.

Therefore:

- no side text beside the wallet/card
- no cramped left/right text columns
- no writing lines or user prompts around the card
- no workbook, scrapbook, diary, or record-book behaviour
- authored book content must sit on separate pages

## Locked first-edition world ritual

Each world receives **4 pages** arranged as **two double-page spreads**.

The locked ritual anatomy is:

**Gate → Meaning → Reveal → World Crest**

World names, provisional place names, World Crest names, and feeling lines are recorded in:

### Spread 1 — Gate + Meaning

This spread provides book-first credibility.

**Page 1: The Gate**

Former internal name: quiet world entry page.

- world title
- world subtitle/place name
- restrained world-specific illustration/texture
- crest or symbol treatment
- same palette and design language as the world
- no card language
- no user prompt
- no writing field

**Page 2: The Meaning**

Former internal name: authored world reading page.

- proper standalone prose
- the feeling, place, atmosphere, and meaning of the world
- written as book content, not card-selection instruction
- helps the customer instinctively understand what belongs in this world
- no fill-in prompts
- no lines
- no tick boxes
- no card-led wording

### Spread 2 — Reveal + World Crest

This spread is the hero product moment.

**Page 3: The Reveal**

Former internal name: full world illustration page.

- full cinematic emotional landscape
- the main visual world reveal
- strong enough to work without any card inserted
- designed as the marketing/customer “money shot” when paired with the World Crest page

**Page 4: The World Crest**

Former internal name: centred card display page.

- finished world-specific Crest page design
- centred personal display area
- minimal title/subtitle only if needed
- subtle world echo, texture, border, particles, or atmospheric detail
- no side text
- no long prompt
- no writing field
- no storage/inventory language

## Why this order is locked

This order gives ApexCrest both requirements:

1. **Book-first credibility** — the world begins with atmosphere and authored meaning before any display moment appears.
2. **Premium hero impact** — the chosen card still sits opposite the full world illustration, creating the strongest customer and marketing spread.

The World Crest page must not be the first or only meaningful page in the chapter.

The Reveal must face the World Crest because that is the strongest emotional product moment.

## Visual register rule

Each world uses two intensity registers, not two different styles.

### Quiet register

Used on The Gate and The Meaning.

It should be restrained, readable, premium, and world-specific.

For Ember, this means ash, charcoal, ember flecks, copper linework, faint volcanic forms, smoke, and dark warm texture.

### Hero register

Used on The Reveal and The World Crest.

It should be cinematic, atmospheric, and more intense, while still using the same palette, typography, border logic, and world identity.

A quiet spread may be a pattern interrupt in intensity, but not in style.

## Locked rule

Each world receives:

- one primary world identity
- one emotional theme
- one Gate page
- one Meaning page
- one Reveal page
- one World Crest page
- one centred personal display area inside the World Crest page
- controlled page echoes across the chapter

## Why 10 Crests

10 World Crest pages is the correct first-edition limit because it avoids the product becoming:

- a binder
- a storage album
- a card-mounting product
- physically bulky when full
- repetitive in page rhythm

The World Crest pages must feel selective.

ApexCrest is for the cards that earned their place.

## Book-first structure

The book must contain substantial illustration and authored world text beyond the World Crest pages.

These pages exist to make the book feel complete and valuable even before any cards are added.

The test is simple:

**If every card were removed, would the world still feel like a finished illustrated book chapter?**

If no, redesign.

## Target page count

Current first-edition target:

- **64 pages preferred**
- **56 pages minimum fallback only if production cost requires it**
- **10 World Crest/display pages maximum**
- 64 pages with 10 World Crest/display pages = **15.6% display-led pages**
- 56 pages with 10 World Crest/display pages = **17.9% display-led pages**
- display-led pages must remain clearly below 25% of the total page count
- additional intro, world map, transition, and closing pages must support book-first character

The previous 48–56 page target is superseded.

48 pages is no longer the preferred first-edition structure. It may only be reconsidered after print-cost review and VAT specialist review.

Avoid:

- 20-page structure
- 40-page structure
- 48-page structure as default
- any structure where display-led pages reach 25% or more of the publication

## 64-page structure

Preferred first-edition structure:

- 8 pages front matter
- 40 pages world chapters
- 16 pages back matter / closing book content

Total:

**8 + 40 + 16 = 64 pages**

## World Crest page rule

World Crest pages are special moments, not repeated storage positions.

No additional Crest/display pages may be added without reopening the product architecture decision.

The World Crest page may include:

- World Crest name
- place subtitle
- small ApexCrest mark
- subtle world echo
- centred personal display area
- feeling line

The World Crest page must not include:

- long text beside the card
- “write here” areas
- prompts for the customer to fill in
- diary/scrapbook/record-book features
- storage/inventory wording
- card checklist language

## Public language rule

Prefer:

- The Gate
- The Meaning
- The Reveal
- Ember Crest / Coral Crest / Forest Crest etc.
- World Crest
- Crest page
- personal display space
- earned place

Use internally where necessary:

- personal card display page
- centred display page
- wallet area
- display area

Avoid customer-facing use of:

- card slot
- holder
- wallet page
- album pocket
- storage slot
- card organiser

Internal production notes may use practical language where needed, but customer-facing copy must remain book-first and Crest-led.
