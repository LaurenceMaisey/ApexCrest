# First Edition 64-Page Book Map

## Status

Locked preferred first-edition build map.

Date locked: 2026-06-27

## Core decision

The preferred first edition is a **64-page A5 hardback illustrated collector-world book**.

The 64-page format is now preferred because it gives ApexCrest stronger book-first substance and places the 10 card display pages clearly below the picture-card/stamp-album risk threshold.

## Page-count lock

Preferred:

- **64 pages total**
- **10 worlds**
- **10 centred personal card display pages maximum**
- **10 card display pages = 15.6% of total pages**

Minimum fallback only if production cost requires it:

- **56 pages total**
- **10 card display pages = 17.9% of total pages**

Avoid:

- 48 pages as the preferred structure
- 40 pages or fewer
- any structure where card display pages reach 25% or more of the publication

## Book architecture

The 64-page book is built from:

- 8 pages front matter
- 40 pages world chapters
- 16 pages back matter / closing book content

Total:

**8 + 40 + 16 = 64 pages**

## World chapter rhythm

Each world uses 4 pages / 2 spreads:

1. quiet world entry page
2. authored world reading page
3. full world illustration page
4. centred card display page

The full illustration must face the centred card display page.

The card display page must not contain side text, writing fields, memory prompts, checklist boxes, storage language, or scrapbook behaviour.

## 64-page pagination map

### Front matter — 8 pages

**Pages 1–2 — Opening identity spread**

- Page 1: ApexCrest mark / half-title
- Page 2: Title page and locked line

Locked line:

**Some cards deserve more than a binder. They deserve a Crest.**

**Pages 3–4 — Book-first introduction spread**

- Page 3: imprint / publication note / small crest detail
- Page 4: opening authored essay on meaning, collecting, and earned place

No card instructions.

**Pages 5–6 — The Crest principle spread**

- Page 5: short authored text on what a Crest means
- Page 6: visual crest system / symbolic mark page

Crest must mean book identity and symbolic recognition, not mechanical card insertion.

**Pages 7–8 — Ten Worlds map spread**

- Page 7: map/list of the ten worlds
- Page 8: illustrated world-route / emotional landscape overview

This spread must sell the book as a journey through worlds, not as a card-capacity product.

### Back matter / closing book content — 16 pages

Back matter must be genuine book content, not extra card storage space.

No extra card display pages may be added here.

**Pages 49–50 — The Ten Worlds in miniature**

- illustrated emblem gallery
- short authored world summaries
- no card checklist
- no tick boxes

**Pages 51–52 — Collector meaning essay**

- authored reflection on why collectors preserve, remember, and honour certain objects
- no user prompts
- no fill-in fields

**Pages 53–54 — The Crest as recognition**

- brand/world philosophy page
- explains Crest as symbolic recognition and book identity
- does not define Crest as insertion or storage

**Pages 55–56 — Care of the book and display pages**

- practical care guidance written as book/product care
- avoid storage-capacity language
- avoid “protect your collection” framing
- no separate sleeve/wallet sales framing

**Pages 57–58 — Closing illustrated spread**

- quiet visual close across the emotional landscapes
- should feel like a finished illustrated book ending

**Pages 59–60 — Closing essay / earned place**

- final authored text
- reinforces meaning, selectivity, and earned place
- no card-management language

**Pages 61–62 — Collected marks / crest gallery**

- visual emblem treatment
- no writable areas
- no checklist functionality

**Pages 63–64 — Colophon / final mark**

- production note, edition mark, final ApexCrest seal
- can include copyright/manufacturing note
- final page should feel designed, not blank

## VAT-safe rationale

The 64-page map supports the book-first position because:

- 54 pages are not card-display pages;
- only 10 pages are card-display-led;
- the card-display pages represent 15.6% of the total publication;
- every world has 3 non-card pages and 1 card-display page;
- front and back matter add genuine authored/illustrated book content;
- the book should feel complete without cards inserted.

## Design guardrails

Every spread must pass the standalone book test:

**If every card were removed, would this still feel like a finished illustrated book?**

If no, redesign.

## Prohibited additions

Do not add:

- extra card display pages
- card inventory pages
- checklist pages
- writing fields
- memory prompts
- blank scrapbook pages
- card-rating pages
- “insert card here” instructions as primary page content
- binder/storage/album language

## Final rule

The 64-page book must read as a complete illustrated collector-world book first.

The card display pages are selective personal display moments inside that book.
