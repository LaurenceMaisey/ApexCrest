# Visual Harmony Specification

## Status

Current source-of-truth visual direction.

## Core visual idea

ApexCrest is built from **emotional landscapes**.

Each world is a premium atmospheric environment expressing a dominant collector feeling.

The artwork must feel universal across TCGs rather than belonging to any one franchise.

## Universal illustration style

**Premium atmospheric collector-book illustration.**

The style should sit between:

- Pokémon brightness
- Magic fantasy depth
- Lorcana storybook warmth
- One Piece adventure energy
- Yu-Gi-Oh dramatic intensity

But copy none of them.

## Style mix

- 60% illustrated storybook atmosphere
- 25% cinematic environment art
- 15% premium editorial design

## Mandatory rules

### 1. Environment only

No characters.
No creatures.
No franchise lookalikes.
No trainers.
No heroes.
No villains.
No mascots.

Use environments, light, weather, atmosphere, path, texture, and symbolic place.

### 2. Match mood, not franchise

Do not create a Pokémon-style, Disney-style, Magic-style, anime-style, or Yu-Gi-Oh-style illustration.

Match:

- colour temperature
- mood
- lighting
- atmosphere
- emotional energy

### 3. Book-first hierarchy

The illustration must be strong enough to justify the page even before a card is inserted.

The card enhances the world.
The world must not depend on the card.

### 4. Controlled right-page echo

The right page must include a subtle echo of the world:

- border motif
- corner motif
- soft texture
- faded wash
- small illustrated strip
- atmospheric particles

It must not become a full second illustration unless deliberately required.

### 5. Premium restraint

Avoid:

- cartoon outlines
- game UI
- fantasy clutter
- fake luxury overload
- excessive gold
- childish illustration
- direct franchise styling

## Compatibility test

Every artwork must plausibly sit beside cards from:

- Pokémon
- Magic: The Gathering
- Disney Lorcana
- One Piece
- Yu-Gi-Oh
- future TCGs

If it only works for one franchise, reject it.

## Golden rule

The viewer should feel:

**This card belongs here.**

Not:

**This artwork was made for that franchise.**
