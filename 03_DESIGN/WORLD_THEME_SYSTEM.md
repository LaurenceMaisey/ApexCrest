# World Theme System

## Status

Locked current production map.

ApexCrest is not 10 fantasy worlds.

ApexCrest is 10 emotional landscapes expressed as premium atmospheric environments.

The goal is not to create a nice art book.

The goal is to create **10 instantly recognisable emotional worlds** that form a category-defining visual system.

## Master differentiation principle

No two worlds may share the same primary landscape archetype, motion language, shape language, or emotional role.

If the worlds begin to collapse into repeated mountains, valleys, dramatic skies, paths or glowing horizons, the design has failed.

## Anchor flexibility principle

Landscape archetype and dominant emotion are hard constraints.

Shape language and hero objects are identity anchors.

They should guide recognition, composition and recurring visual language, but they must not become forced literal props in every image.

A hero object may be:

- foregrounded
- backgrounded
- partially visible
- implied through structure
- echoed on the right page
- represented through texture, silhouette or border language

The purpose is recognisable identity, not visual repetition.

## 1. Ember Tribute

- Dominant emotion: Passion
- Landscape archetype: Volcanic valley
- Shape language: Jagged
- Motion language: Burning, rising, transforming
- Hero object: Molten fissure
- Visual language: lava, magma, ash, fire rivers, ember glow, molten cracks
- Echo: drifting embers, ash flecks, molten border detail
- Avoid: dragons, battle scenes, creature silhouettes

## 2. Lagoon Celebration

- Dominant emotion: Joy
- Landscape archetype: Tropical water haven
- Shape language: Horizontal
- Motion language: Rippling, flowing, sparkling
- Hero object: Shell / coral edge
- Visual language: water, coral, shells, coves, palms, sunlight on water
- Echo: shells, coral, ripples, waterline border
- Avoid: pirate clutter, overt adventure props

## 3. Forest Remembrance

- Dominant emotion: Memory
- Landscape archetype: Ancient woodland
- Shape language: Vertical
- Motion language: Slow growth, filtered light, endurance
- Hero object: Roots
- Visual language: roots, vines, old trees, moss, ruins, streams, woodland path
- Echo: vines, roots, leaves, soft woodland texture
- Avoid: fairy woodland, overly cute treatment

## 4. Moonlight Discovery

- Dominant emotion: Mystery
- Landscape archetype: Moonlit ruins
- Shape language: Archways
- Motion language: Reflection, mist, hidden passage, quiet reveal
- Hero object: Ruined arch
- Visual language: stone arches, reflections, moon pools, hidden paths, violet mist
- Echo: moonlit branches, violet mist, silver linework
- Avoid: horror, gothic overload

## 5. Prism Harmony

- Dominant emotion: Beauty
- Landscape archetype: Valley of Light
- Shape language: Terraced
- Motion language: Refraction, soft convergence, colour harmony
- Hero object: Reflective terraces
- Visual language: terraces, waterfalls, reflective pools, refraction, soft rainbow haze, light gathering
- Echo: refracted light, pastel rays, crystal dust
- Avoid: crystal kingdom, gemstone cave, rainbow explosion, mountains as primary subject

Prism is a beautiful valley where light gathers.

It is not a place made of crystals.

## 6. Storm Ascent

- Dominant emotion: Challenge
- Landscape archetype: Storm coast / lightning pass
- Shape language: Diagonal
- Motion language: Violent sky, wind, waves, pressure, movement
- Hero object: Lighthouse / lightning pass
- Visual language: cliffs, crashing sea, lightning, rain, wind, storm clouds, broken light
- Echo: storm clouds, lightning veins, rain texture
- Avoid: snowy mountains, frozen peaks, heroic summit framing

Storm should feel like movement and danger.

## 7. Frost Resolve

- Dominant emotion: Endurance
- Landscape archetype: Frozen lake / ice valley
- Shape language: Flat
- Motion language: Stillness, silence, breath, cold
- Hero object: Frozen tree / ice sheet
- Visual language: ice sheets, frozen water, frost forests, pale light, silence, cold air
- Echo: frost crystals, frozen branches, ice texture
- Avoid: heroic mountain climb, epic summit framing, storm drama

Frost should feel quiet, not epic.

## 8. Celestial Wonder

- Dominant emotion: Awe
- Landscape archetype: Night lake beneath the Milky Way
- Shape language: Curved
- Motion language: Upward gaze, vastness, cosmic reflection
- Hero object: Night lake / Orion sky
- Visual language: water, stars, galaxy reflection, shoreline, Orion’s Belt, Milky Way, shooting star
- Echo: stars, galaxy dust, Milky Way bleed
- Avoid: astrology wheels, observatories, sci-fi structures, giant planets, mountain dominance

Celestial is awe through scale.

It is not astronomy clutter.

## 9. Summit Achievement

- Dominant emotion: Triumph
- Landscape archetype: Golden mountain path above clouds
- Shape language: Triangular
- Motion language: Upward path, arrival, sunrise, earned height
- Hero object: Stone stair
- Visual language: ascent, stairways, ridges, arrival, sunrise, stonework, alpine plants, golden mist
- Echo: stonework, alpine plants, golden mist
- Avoid: ice/frost dominance, storm darkness, frozen mountain treatment

Summit is the only world allowed to be primarily mountain-focused.

Summit should feel like arrival.

## 10. Sanctuary Legacy

- Dominant emotion: Belonging
- Landscape archetype: Warm refuge / hidden hall / candlelit garden
- Shape language: Enclosed
- Motion language: Still warmth, safety, memory, shelter
- Hero object: Lantern
- Visual language: hall, garden, lanterns, shelter, ivy, warm gold, soft wood, refuge
- Echo: ivy, lanterns, warm light, soft wood texture
- Avoid: tomb heaviness, temple heaviness, dark shrine energy

## Mandatory anti-repetition rules

### 1. Environment Diversity Law

No two worlds may share the same primary landscape archetype.

Only Summit may be primarily mountain-focused.

### 2. Shape Language Law

Every world must be recognisable by silhouette and form, not just colour.

Shape language is a compositional guide, not a literal geometry requirement.

### 3. One Hero Object Law

Every world must include, imply, or echo its locked hero object.

The hero object is not required to be the main foreground subject.

### 4. Default AI Rejection Rule

Reject any generation that defaults to:

- generic fantasy valley
- dramatic mountain range
- sky plus path
- glowing horizon
- castle/city/kingdom
- over-detailed fantasy clutter

unless that specific structure is explicitly locked for the world.

## Fast differentiation checklist

- Ember = volcanic valley, jagged, molten fissure
- Lagoon = tropical water haven, horizontal, shell/coral edge
- Forest = ancient woodland, vertical, roots
- Moonlight = moonlit ruins, archways, ruined arch
- Prism = Valley of Light, terraced, reflective terraces
- Storm = storm coast, diagonal, lighthouse/lightning pass
- Frost = frozen lake, flat, frozen tree/ice sheet
- Celestial = night lake, curved, Orion sky
- Summit = golden mountain path, triangular, stone stair
- Sanctuary = warm refuge, enclosed, lantern

## Approval hierarchy

Hard pass/fail:

1. Dominant emotion
2. Landscape archetype
3. No-character rule
4. Universal TCG compatibility
5. Book-first / VAT-safe hierarchy
6. Clear differentiation from other worlds

Flexible but required as recognisable signals:

1. Shape language
2. Hero object
3. Right-page echo motif
4. Secondary visual details

## Final rule

The book must not become ten beautiful fantasy landscapes.

It must become ten instantly recognisable emotional worlds.
