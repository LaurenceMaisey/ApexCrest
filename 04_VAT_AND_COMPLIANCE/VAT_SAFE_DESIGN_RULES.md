# VAT-Safe Design Rules

## Status

Current design guardrail, not tax advice.

A written VAT specialist opinion is still required before relying on zero-rating.

## Core position

ApexCrest must be designed and marketed as a **premium illustrated collector book**.

It must not be designed or marketed as:

- an album
- a picture-card album
- a binder
- a card holder
- a storage book
- a display accessory
- a scrapbook
- a record book
- a stationery item

## HMRC risk principle

HMRC guidance treats picture-card and stamp albums as standard-rated unless they include:

1. substantial reading matter complete in itself; and
2. no more than 25% of the album set aside for mounting cards or stamps.

ApexCrest must be built to support the argument that it is a book first and that card placement is incidental.

## Mandatory design constraints

### Must do

- The product must be a hardback illustrated book.
- It must contain substantial reading matter and illustrations complete in themselves.
- The book must still feel complete and valuable without any cards inserted.
- Card placement must be secondary and incidental.
- Total card-mounting area must remain clearly below 25% of the whole publication.
- Both left and right pages must contain book content, whether illustration, prose, theme text, narrative, or editorial treatment.
- Right pages may use subtle illustration: borders, corner motifs, faded washes, side strips, atmospheric particles, and frame details.
- The product must be marketed as a premium illustrated collector book.

### Must not do

- Do not make one page the illustration page and the other page simply the card page.
- Do not make the card slot the main feature of the spread.
- Do not use large blank writing areas.
- Do not make the product feel like a scrapbook, record book, form, diary, or stationery item.
- Do not use “insert card here” as the main page purpose.
- Do not sell or describe the product as a card-mounting system with decoration added.
- Do not use album, binder, holder, storage, display accessory, or scrapbook wording in customer-facing copy.

## Layout direction

Preferred page rhythm:

- full illustration pages
- reading/story pages
- limited card-placement pages
- interspersed non-card anchor pages

First edition should target:

- 10 card pages maximum
- additional illustration/story pages
- approximately 38–44 pages total

## Reading matter requirement

Every world should include meaningful text that can be read independently of the inserted card.

Avoid simple prompts only.

Good reading matter includes:

- collector philosophy
- short world essays
- emotional explanations
- collecting memories
- reflections on rarity, nostalgia, achievement, chase, beauty, wonder, and legacy

## Product language guardrail

Safe language:

- premium illustrated collector book
- illustrated collector book
- hardback illustrated book
- collector story book
- book of illustrated worlds

Risky language:

- album
- binder
- card storage
- card holder
- display accessory
- mounting system
- record book
- scrapbook

## Final rule

If the product would still feel complete without cards inserted, it is moving in the right direction.

If it feels empty, unfinished, or primarily waiting for cards, redesign it.
