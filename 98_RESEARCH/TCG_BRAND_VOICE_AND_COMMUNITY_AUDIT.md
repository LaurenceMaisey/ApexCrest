# TCG Brand Voice & Community Audit

## Status

Stage 2A — Evidence Collection v0.1.

This file records raw research findings for the ApexCrest Brand Voice & Editorial Style Guide workflow.

This is **not yet the final synthesis** and is **not yet the ApexCrest voice guide**.

## Method

This stage audits official brand language and high-level collector/community language signals across major fantasy trading-card games.

The purpose is to collect evidence before making final recommendations.

## Source handling

Primary sources are preferred where available.

Secondary summaries are used only where official source text is limited or difficult to parse.

This file should be expanded in later passes with more official product-page examples, social/community examples, and legal review notes.

---

# Executive Evidence Summary

Across the audited brands, several recurring territories appear:

- collecting
- favourite characters/cards
- deck-building
- adventure
- community
- competition
- worlds/settings
- rarity/chase
- powerful cards
- beautiful artwork
- organised play
- beginner accessibility

The strongest repeated cross-brand language areas are:

- collection
- journey/adventure
- world
- story
- powerful
- favourite
- community
- championship/event/play
- rare/legendary/special
- art/artwork

The most legally sensitive areas are franchise-specific role terms, slogans, character names, card-mechanic names, set names and distinctive world terminology.

# Stage 2A Holding Conclusions

The evidence suggests ApexCrest should not copy any one brand voice.

ApexCrest should likely combine:

- Pokémon’s accessibility and emotional collecting
- Magic’s world depth and mature sense of story
- Lorcana’s warmth, beauty and artwork appreciation
- Yu-Gi-Oh!’s respect for iconic cards and legacy
- One Piece’s sense of journey, but with less energy and less franchise-coded adventure
- Flesh and Blood’s honour/prestige, softened away from competition
- Final Fantasy’s elegant dreamlike restraint

This is not yet the final recommendation. It is a working observation to be tested in Stage 2B synthesis.

# Stage 2A Limitations

This pass is evidence-led but not yet exhaustive.
